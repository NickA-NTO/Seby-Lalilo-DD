Was Seby Placed at the Right Level?

Was the content too difficult for him?

Not Sustained

Across the full period, Seby's overall accuracy was 79%. Session 1 came in at 73% — the lower boundary of the productive learning zone — with Sessions 2 and 3 both at 80%. The accuracy zone breakdown shows that 91% of total time was spent in the productive challenge zone, the target range for adaptive learning programs.

August comprised 4 days of work at 67% accuracy. In September, 3 of 14 sessions fell below the productive threshold at 67–68%. From October onward, performance was consistently within the productive range. The data does not support characterising the overall placement as too difficult.

Does difficulty explain the off-task behavior?

Not Supported

Research analysis of study footage documented that 50–70% of recorded app time was idle or unproductive. The waste-adjusted XP/min figures show what Seby produced when he was actually engaged: 1.32 in Session 2, 1.04 in Session 3, and 1.06 overall. If content difficulty were the primary cause of disengagement, we would expect struggling performance even during engaged time — low accuracy, low XP output, or both. The data shows the opposite: when Seby was working, he was working at a productive rate.

Session 1's waste-adjusted rate was lower at 0.39, consistent with the early period presenting more of a challenge. However, Sessions 2 and 3 show a student performing capably when focused. Combined with the finding that 91% of total time was spent in the productive accuracy zone, the available evidence does not support content difficulty as the driver of the time waste.

Did Seby move through the content too quickly?

Not Supported

Seby advanced 0.66 grade equivalents in 5.75 months against a target of 1.15 grade equivalents — reaching 57% of the expected pace. The data shows movement below the target rate, not above it.

Accounting for the documented 50% time waste (conservative figure), the effective learning rate projects to 1.32 grade equivalents over the same period — 15% above the target. The below-target pace is attributable to time efficiency, not to the content being too easy or the progression being too fast.
